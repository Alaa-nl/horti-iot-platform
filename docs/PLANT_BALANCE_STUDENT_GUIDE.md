## ğŸ“š Key Concepts Explained Simply

### Photosynthesis (Making Food)
- **What it is:** Plants use sunlight, CO2 from air, and water to make sugar (their food)
- **Think of it like:** A kitchen where plants cook their meals
- **What affects it:**
  - More light = more cooking (up to a point)
  - More CO2 = more ingredients
  - Right temperature (20-25Â°C) = perfect cooking conditions
  - Good humidity = kitchen doors (stomata) stay open

### Respiration (Using Energy)
- **What it is:** Plants "breathe" and burn sugar for energy, just like we do!
- **Think of it like:** A car burning fuel to run
- **What affects it:**
  - Higher temperature = burns more fuel
  - Night time = still burning fuel (no photosynthesis)

### Net Assimilation (Plant Profit)
- **What it is:** What's left after subtracting energy use from energy production
- **Think of it like:** Income - Expenses = Savings
- **Formula:** Photosynthesis - Respiration = Growth

### Transpiration
- **What it is:** Water evaporating from leaves
- **Think of it like:** Plant sweating to cool down
- **Benefits:**
  - Cools the plant
  - Pulls nutrients up from roots
  - Keeps stomata open for CO2

### Root Water Uptake
- **What it is:** How roots drink water from soil
- **Best conditions:**
  - Root temperature: 18-22Â°C
  - Not too wet (drowning)
  - Not too dry (thirsty)

### VPD (Vapor Pressure Deficit)
- **Simple explanation:** How thirsty the air is
- **Optimal range:** 0.8-1.2 kPa
- **Too low (<0.5):** Air too humid, disease risk
- **Too high (>2.0):** Air too dry, plants stressed

### PAR Light
- **What it is:** The light plants can actually use (not all light is useful!)
- **Measured in:** Î¼mol/mÂ²/s
- **Good levels:**
  - Low light plants: 100-200
  - Most vegetables: 400-600

### CO2 Levels
- **Normal air:** 400 ppm
- **Enriched greenhouse:** 800-1000 ppm
- **Effect:** Double CO2 can increase growth by 30%!
